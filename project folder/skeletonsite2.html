Lures and Tactics


This is an orange chartruce crankbait

There are thousands of different lures out there as designed basically to do the same thing.. catch fish! There are some key differences though that will be explained here to make your fishing experience easier.
To Start, for intermediate anglers there are two types of lures that should always be in your tackle box.
1. Crank baits
a. What these lures are is a piece of plastic shaped to resemble a prey fish. They usually have clear plastic lip on the front that helps it dive and shake in the water
2. Soft Plastics
a. Soft plastics are rubber like material shaped into usually worms and crawfish and they are cheap, effective and catch many different kinds of fish.

Targeting Species

In this video, it goes over many more tips to catch Largemouth Bass.

There are many different fish for intermediate anglers. One of the most popular fish species for intermediate anglers to catch is large mouth bass. This is freshwater species that is in the northern areas of America. To catch one, bass mainly eat smaller prey fish and pretty much anything that fits in their mouths so getting them to bite relatively easy.

There are many fish with similar eating habits as bass in both fresh and salt water. Most of these fish will bite on crank baits and lures too.
